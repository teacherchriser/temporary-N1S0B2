Hello, do you want to listen a little bit about Asian music? Music plays a vital role in daily life across these regions. In China, people listen to Mandopop and C-Pop, which combine traditional melodies with modern beats. Many citizens enjoy folk music during festivals, and young people prefer electronic dance music in the big cities. In Singapore, the music scene reflects its multicultural identity. Residents hear songs in English, Mandarin, Malay, and Tamil. Local radio stations play a mix of international hits and "Xinyao" (Which is Singaporean Chinese folk-pop), which remains a proud part of their heritage.

The Guzheng, a traditional Chinese zither, originated more than 2,500 years ago during the Qin Dynasty. Initially, the instrument had only five strings made of silk. Musicians played it in royal courts to express nature and poetry. In the 20th century, the design changed to include 21 strings made of metal. Similarly, in Singapore, the "Xinyao" movement started in the 80s and Students composed simple songs about friendship and local life and these melodies became the soundtrack of a generation.

Additionally, many independent artists are experimenting with "Chinoiserie" (mixing hip-hop with traditional Chinese instruments like the pipa), and this unique sound is capturing the attention of the global audience.
